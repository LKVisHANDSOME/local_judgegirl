55. The Robots

I'm a slow walker, but I never walk backwards.

Task Description

Write a program to predict the fate of two robots. Suppose we have two robots running in an MM (horizontal) by NN (vertical) unit grid. Both will move at the speed of one square per time step. The first robot carries F1F1 amount of fuel and the second robot carries F2F2. If a robot runs out of fuel, it will stop at that square. Moving to a new square requires one unit of fuel. During the first N1N1 time steps, the first robot R1R1 will move to the north; then, it will move towards the east during the next E1E1 time steps. The first robot will repeat this pattern until it runs out of fuel. The second robot R2R2 will move a little bit differently. During the first E2E2 time steps, the second robot R2R2 will move to the east; then, it will move towards the north during the next N2N2 time steps. Again the second robot will repeat this pattern until it runs out of fuel. If either robot moves "out of bound," it will "wrap around" and reappear (by sort of magic) on the other side of the field. For example, if M=7M=7 and N=6N=6 and a robot at (5,5)(5,5) goes north, it will reappear at (5,0)(5,0). In addition, if two robots move into the same square, they explode. Now given the starting position of the first robot at (X1,Y1)(X1,Y1), and the second robot at (X2,Y2)(X2,Y2), and the amount of fuel they carry (F1F1 and F2F2), determine whether the two robots will explode or not.

Input

There is only one line of inputs that contains M,;N,;X1,;Y1,;E1,;N1,;F1,;X2,;Y2,;E2,;N2,;F2M,;N,;X1,;Y1,;E1,;N1,;F1,;X2,;Y2,;E2,;N2,;F2, with the following constraints.

  • 0<N,;M<100000<N,;M<10000
  • 0≤X1,;X2<M0≤X1,;X2<M
  • 0≤Y1,;Y2<N0≤Y1,;Y2<N
  • (X1,Y1) is not (X2,Y2)(X1,Y1) is not (X2,Y2)
  • 0<N1,;E1,;N2,;E20<N1,;E1,;N2,;E2
  • 0≤F1,;F2≤100000≤F1,;F2≤10000

Output

There are two cases of output. If two robots explode, output robots explode at time T, where TT is the time they explode. Otherwise, output robots will not explode.

Sample input

7 6 2 0 9 2 100 3 5 2 7 100

Sample output

robots explode at time 5

Sample input

7 6 2 0 9 2 6 3 5 2 7 0

Sample output

robots will not explode
