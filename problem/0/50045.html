50045. Mines and Black Holes

I'm a slow walker, but I never walk backwards.

Task Description

A spaceship is traveling in a 3D space. Given the initial position of the spaceship and a series of NN instructions, simulate the movements of the spaceship and output its positions periodically.

The initial position of spaceship is (x0,y0,z0)(x0,y0,z0). It will move according to instructions. Each instruction is a number from 11 to 66, indicating the direction the spaceship should go. Let the current position of spaceship be (x,y,z)(x,y,z):

  • 11 - The spaceship moves from (x,y,z)(x,y,z) to (x+1,y,z)(x+1,y,z)
  • 22 - The spaceship moves from (x,y,z)(x,y,z) to (x−1,y,z)(x−1,y,z)
  • 33 - The spaceship moves from (x,y,z)(x,y,z) to (x,y+1,z)(x,y+1,z)
  • 44 - The spaceship moves from (x,y,z)(x,y,z) to (x,y−1,z)(x,y−1,z)
  • 55 - The spaceship moves from (x,y,z)(x,y,z) to (x,y,z+1)(x,y,z+1)
  • 66 - The spaceship moves from (x,y,z)(x,y,z) to (x,y,z−1)(x,y,z−1)

There are a mine and a black hole in the space. The mine is located at (xm,ym,zm)(xm,ym,zm) and the black hole is located at (xb,yb,zb)(xb,yb,zb).

The distance between two points in space is the sum of the absolute values of the differences between their coordinates in all three dimensions. That is, the distance between two points p=(xp,yp,zp)p=(xp,yp,zp) and q=(xq,yq,zq)q=(xq,yq,zq) is defined as

dist(p,q)=|xp−xq|+|yp−yq|+|zp−zq|;.dist(p,q)=|xp−xq|+|yp−yq|+|zp−zq|;.

Both mine and block hole will attract the spaceship within a certain range. When the distance between the spaceship and the mine is smaller than 1010 units, the spaceship will be attracted to the mine and explode at location (xm,ym,zm)(xm,ym,zm). The spaceship cannot travel after the explosion, so you can ignore any further instructions. When the distance between the spaceship and the black hole is smaller than 1010 units, the spaceship will be attracted to the black hole, the black hole will then disappear and the spaceship will continue traveling from (xb,yb,zb)(xb,yb,zb).

The attraction of the mine is greater than the attraction of the black hole. Therefore, after a movement, if the distances between the spaceship to both the mine and the black hole are smaller than 1010 units, the spaceship will be attracted to the mine and explode.

Given the initial position of the spaceship (x0,y0,z0)(x0,y0,z0), the position of the mine (xm,ym,zm)(xm,ym,zm), the position of the black hole (xb,yb,zb)(xb,yb,zb) and a series of NN movement instructions m1,m2,⋯,mNm1,m2,⋯,mN, output the position of the spaceship every 55 instructions, i.e., output the position after m5,m10,m15,⋯,m⌊N5⌋×5m5,m10,m15,⋯,m⌊N5⌋×5. Note that if the spaceship explodes after mkmk, you do not need to output anything after the explosion even if kk is a multiple of 55.

For the given three points (x0,y0,z0)(x0,y0,z0), (xm,ym,zm)(xm,ym,zm) and (xb,yb,zb)(xb,yb,zb), the distance between any two of them will not be smaller than 1010 units.

Input Format

There are four lines in the input file. The first line contains three integers x0,y0,z0x0,y0,z0, representing the initial position of the spaceship. The second line contains six integers xm,ym,zm,xb,yb,zbxm,ym,zm,xb,yb,zb, representing the positions of the mine and the black hole. The third line contains a single integer NN, representing the number of movement instructions. Finally, the last line contains NN integers m1,m2,⋯,mNm1,m2,⋯,mN, representing the NN movement instructions.

If the spaceship doesn't explode, output ⌊N5⌋⌊N5⌋ lines. The ii-th line contains three integers xi,yi,zixi,yi,zi, representing the position of the spaceship after mi×5mi×5.
