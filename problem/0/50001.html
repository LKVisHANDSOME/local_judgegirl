50001. Vehicle

I'm a slow walker, but I never walk backwards.

  • Status
  • Statistics
  • Solution

Problem description

You are on an automatic vehicle. This vehicle will automatically moves to a new location every hour according to the follow rules. If you are in position (x,y)(x,y), then it will move in the direction decided by |ax+by|%4|ax+by|%4. If the result is 00, 11, 22, 33, then the vehicle will go north, east, south and west. For example, if we start at (1,1)(1,1) and aa is 33, and bb is 22, then we will go east to (2,1)(2,1) in the next hour.

We will collect gold in during this traveling. The amount of gold in location (x,y)(x,y) is |cx+dy|%e|cx+dy|%e. For example, if we are in location (1,1)(1,1) and cc is 33, dd is 44, and ee is 55, then we can collect 22 units of gold. In addition, the gold in a location is replenishable, That means if we visit the location (1,1)(1,1) three times, we can collect 66 units of gold.

The range of our traveling is limited by the fuel of the vehicle. Every hour the vehicle will consume one unit of fuel to move to a new location. The amount of fuel you start with is FF. Note that after you move into the new location and run out of fuel, you can still collect the gold in that location. For example, if you only have 11 unit of fuel and go from AA to BB, you can collect gold from bothboth locations AA and BB.

There are two transporters located at positions (p,q)(p,q) and (r,s)(r,s). If you move into position (p,q)(p,q), you will be immediately transported to (r,s)(r,s) withoutwithout consuming any fuel. Similarly if you move into (r,s)(r,s) you will go to (p,q)(p,q). There is nono gold in the positions where the two transporters are located. These two transporters are notnot next to each other, and you will notnot start traveling from any of the transporters. You will be transported even if you do not have fuel.

Your program must simulate the vehicle movement after you start from location (x,y)(x,y) and report the amount of gold you collected, and the position where you run stop. All numbers aa, bb, cc, dd, ee, xx, yy, and FF are between 00 and 100100.

Technical Specification and constraints

  • 0≤a,b,c,d,e,x,y,F≤1000≤a,b,c,d,e,x,y,F≤100
  • 20 pt. p,q,r,s>1000p,q,r,s>1000. That is, you do not need to consider the transporters.
  • 80 pt. No restriction on pp, qq, rr, ss, so you might encounter the transporters.

Input Format

There are three lines in the output. The first line has the integers aa, bb, cc, dd, ee, the second line has the integers pp, qq, rr, ss and the last line has the integers xx, yy, FF.

Output Format

The first line print the amount of gold you collected, and the second line print the position where you run out of fuel.

Sample Input 1

1 3 5 7 9
1000 1000 1002 1002
1 1 2

Sample Output

9
0 2

Sample Input 2

1 3 5 7 9
1 2 3 4
1 1 6

Sample Output 2

19
2 4

Hint

The vehicle will go like the following according to the sample input. We start from (1,1)(1,1) and collect (5×1+7×1)%9=3(5×1+7×1)%9=3 units of gold. Then we go north since (1×3+5×7)%4=0(1×3+5×7)%4=0, which means north. That is, we go from (1,1)(1,1) to (1,2)(1,2). Because (1,2)(1,2) has a transport, we end up in (3,4)(3,4). Note that we did not get any gold at either (1,2)(1,2) or (3,4)(3,4). Finally we get 1919 units of gold.
